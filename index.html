Guild War Rules
Each member must read and follow these guild rules:
  • It is required to be active during guild wars. If there is a reason you will not be able to log in, let someone know BEFORE the war begins, and it will be excused. Inactive members during the guild war will be kicked out at the end of the war.
  • We declare war on the hour the guild war starts, and then every 2 hours. Declaring war randomly will get you kicked out at the end of the war.
  • Everyone is required to change their name to RK during guild war. This makes it difficult for opposing guild to identify easy targets. Not changing your name in time for guild war will get you kicked out before guild war.
  • We do not require gems to be spent, but it is appreciated. Respect the choices of your guild mates to gem or not.
Officer ranks distribution
We rank officers by total number of attacks during guild war, not by points, to encourage everyone to attack guardians and sentinels. The ranks are awarded in the following order:
  • Guild Champion (1 seat) - Attributed to the member with the most attacks at the end of guild war.
  • High-commanders (9 seats) - Attributed to the members with the most attacks, following the guild champion, at the end of guild war.
  • High-commanders (2 seats) - Attributed to the top 2 gold contributing members during the last week.

Should I attack different opponents?
Find an opponent you can defeat and keep attacking that opponent. The amount of points awarded for a battle is random and does not decrease with the amount of attacks on a single opponent. Therefore, we recommend to avoid shopping around for new opponents.

Remember, each battle lost is a waste of points for the guild.
Is there a bonus for defeating high-commanders?
Each victory against a high-commander provides an extra 20% bonus points.
What does HC and C means?
  • HC: High-Commander
  • C: Commander
What does 3.2k means?
This is the total strength of a player. It's calculated using the attack + defense of their main character, divided by 1000.

Here is an example for player X with an attack of 1800 and a defense of 1400.
  • Player X total strength = (1800+1400)/1000 = 3200/1000 = 3.2k.

It is a good idea to know in advance your own total strength to better evaluate who you have a better chance of defeating.
